Canonical one-sentence definition

Design knowledge is the explicit, machine-readable body of functions, constraints, rules and relationships that justify and generate a design — the design logic and intent — as distinct from the geometry and properties that merely record its final, realised state.

Slide-ready (tight) version

Design knowledge = the why and how behind a model, not the model itself.
The rules, constraints, parametric relationships and functional intent that produce and govern geometry — formalised so they can be stored, shared, reasoned over and validated, independently of any one tool or file.

Research definition (expanded, with the FBS spine)
In this research design knowledge is framed through the Function–Behaviour–Structure lens (Gero). A BIM deliverable captures Structure — the realised geometry and properties that IFC represents well. Design knowledge is everything upstream of that artefact:

Functional intent — what the design must achieve (spatial program, adjacencies, performance goals).
Constraints and rules — the regulatory, project and disciplinary requirements a valid design must satisfy (e.g. "max building height ≤ 75 m", "habitable rooms ≥ X m² with daylight access"), formalised here as SWRL rules over an ontology.
Relationships and dependencies — the parametric and topological logic that links objects and drives generation (the Grasshopper/grammar logic).
Rationale — the design reasoning that connects intent to form, which conventional handovers discard.

It is defined by three properties that separate it from ordinary BIM data: it is explicit and machine-readable (encoded as ontology + SWRL atoms, not implicit in a node graph or a designer's head), generative and prescriptive (it produces and tests designs, rather than describing a finished one), and tool- and file-independent (it lives in a shared semantic core referenced by all disciplines, not locked inside one platform's export).
In one line for the talk: IFC carries the building's Structure; Design Grammars carries its Design Knowledge — the Function, Behaviour and rules that produced it.
